* Bipartite Systems and Global States
Consider a composite quantum system in $\mathcal{H}_{AB} = \mathcal{H}_A \otimes \mathcal{H}_B$.
For a pure state $|\psi\rangle_{AB}$,
the global density matrix is

$$\rho_{AB} = |\psi\rangle\langle\psi|_{AB} \, .$$

A local observable $M_A$ implies the global measurement operator $M_A \otimes I_B$,
which yields the expectation value

$$\langle M_A \rangle = \, _{AB}\langle\psi | M_A \otimes I_B | \psi\rangle_{AB} = \text{Tr}_{AB}((M_A \otimes I_B)\rho_{AB}) \, .$$

* The Partial Trace
To extract a local description,
trace out subsystem $B$.
Using an orthonormal basis $\{|k\rangle_B\}$ of $\mathcal{H}_B$,
the partial trace is

$$\text{Tr}_B(\rho_{AB}) = \sum_k (I_A \otimes \langle k|_B) \rho_{AB} (I_A \otimes |k\rangle_B) \, .$$

Expanding the global state in a joint basis to reveal the loss of coherence gives

$$\rho_{AB} = \sum_{i,\mu,j,\nu} c_{i\mu} c_{j\nu}^* (|i\rangle\langle j|_A) \otimes (|\mu\rangle\langle\nu|_B) \, .$$

Applying the trace evaluates the inner products on $B$ as

$$\langle k | \mu \rangle \langle \nu | k \rangle = \delta_{k\mu} \delta_{\nu k} \, .$$

For these terms to survive, $\mu = \nu = k$.
Because the basis states are orthogonal,
all off-diagonal cross-terms ($\mu \neq \nu$) vanish,
and the operators of $B$ collapse into scalar weights.

* The Density Operator
The resulting marginal matrix is the reduced density operator

$$\rho_A \equiv \text{Tr}_B(\rho_{AB}) \, .$$

Writing the bipartite pure state in the diagonal basis of $A$,
$|\psi\rangle_{AB} = \sum_{i,\mu} a_{i\mu} |i\rangle_A \otimes |\mu\rangle_B$,
yields the explicit density matrix summation

$$\rho_A = \sum_i p_i |i\rangle\langle i| \, .$$

Because $M_A$ acts exclusively on $A$,
it factors out of the partial trace.
Local expectation values are determined entirely by $\rho_A$,

$$\boxed{ \langle M_A \rangle = \text{Tr}_A(M_A \rho_A) } \, .$$

* The Ensemble Interpretation
By the no-signaling theorem,
local measurements cannot distinguish entanglement from a classical mixture.
Thus, $\rho_A$ takes a spectral decomposition representing a statistical ensemble,

$$\rho_A = \sum_i p_i |\psi_i\rangle\langle\psi_i| \, ,$$

where $p_i$ is the classical probability of occupying the pure state $|\psi_i\rangle$ ($\sum p_i = 1$).
Applying $\rho_A$ to a basis state yields $\rho_A | \psi_k \rangle = p_k | \psi_k \rangle$,
confirming the probabilities $p_i$ are exactly the eigenvalues of $\rho_A$.

* State Purity
The eigenvalue spectrum of $\rho_A$ dictates subsystem purity.
A pure state has exactly one eigenvalue of $p_1 = 1$,
leaving no classical uncertainty and ensuring $\text{Tr}(\rho_A^2) = 1$.
A mixed state distributes probability across multiple non-negative eigenvalues,
creating a statistical mixture where $\text{Tr}(\rho_A^2) < 1$.
Consequently,
a maximally entangled global state yields a maximally mixed local state ($\rho_A = \frac{1}{d}I$),
reflecting total local randomization.
